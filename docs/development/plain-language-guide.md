# Plain Language Guide

## Goal

The goal of the "Simplified View" is to make service information accessible to users with:

- Low literacy (Grade 6-8 reading level)
- Cognitive disabilities
- Stress or crisis states (which lower reading comprehension)
- English as a second language

## Core Principles

### 1. Simple Words

Use everyday language. Avoid bureaucratic jargon.
| Instead of... | Use... |
|---------------|--------|
| Eligibility requirements | Who is this for? |
| Documentation required | What to bring |
| Utilize | Use |
| In the event of | If |

### 2. Short Sentences

- Keep sentences under 15 words.
- One idea per sentence.
- Use active voice ("Call us" not "Calls can be made").

### 3. Clear Formatting

- Use bullet points for lists.
- Use **bold** for key actions.
- Avoid large blocks of text.

## Structure

### Summary (Max 500 chars)

A 1-2 sentence explanation of what the service actually _does_.
_Example: "We provide free food to families in need. You get a box of groceries once a month."_

### How to Use (Max 1000 chars)

Step-by-step instructions on how to access the service.
_Example:_

1. _Call 613-555-0199._
2. _Ask for an appointment._
3. _Bring your ID when you visit._

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Readability score (Flesch-Kincaid) is Grade 8 or lower.
- [ ] No sentences longer than 20 words.
- [ ] Phone numbers are clickable/easy to read.
- [ ] No acronyms without explanation.
