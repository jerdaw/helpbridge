Ontario-Wide Health Services (Non-Crisis, Information & Navigation)

Below is a comprehensive list of free, province-wide health services in Ontario that anyone can access via toll-free phone, and in many cases text or online chat. These services are non-crisis in nature, focusing on health information, advice, and system navigation. (For emergency or crisis situations, call 911 or appropriate crisis lines.)
General Health Advice (Telehealth)

    Health811 (Health Connect Ontario) – Free 24/7 provincial health advice line. By dialing 811 (TTY: 1-800-855-0511) you can speak with a registered nurse for confidential health advice or information
    toronto.ca
    . Also offers a web portal and online chat with advisors. Service is provided in English and French, with interpretation available for 200+ languages. Website: Ontario Health’s Health811 site (Ontario.ca/Health811).

Poison Information

    Ontario Poison Centre / Centre antipoison de l’Ontario – 24/7 expert poison advice line for all ages. Provides emergency poison information and treatment advice in over 170 languages
    ontariopoisoncentre.ca
    ontariopoisoncentre.ca
    . Phone: 1‑800‑268‑9017 or 1‑844‑764‑7669 (toll-free, province-wide)
    ontariopoisoncentre.ca
    ontariopoisoncentre.ca
    . Serves the entire Ontario public (and health professionals) with specialists in poison information on call at all times. Website: ontariopoisoncentre.ca (with resources in English and French).

Mental Health & Addictions (Information & Navigation)

    ConnexOntario – 24/7 mental health, addiction (drug/alcohol), and problem gambling information and referral service (government-funded). Provides free and confidential information about counselling, treatment and support services across Ontario
    torontocentralhealthline.ca
    . Phone (24/7): 1‑866‑531‑2600
    torontocentralhealthline.ca
    . Text: send “CONNEX” to 247247
    torontocentralhealthline.ca
    . Online: live web chat with an Information & Referral Specialist
    torontocentralhealthline.ca
    . Serves all Ontarians (general public and health/service providers) looking for non-crisis support options. Bilingual service (English and French) with 24/7 availability and interpretation in 170+ languages
    torontocentralhealthline.ca
    .

Smoking Cessation & Substance Use Support

    Smokers’ Helpline – Free quit-smoking support service (run by Canadian Cancer Society). Provides confidential telephone coaching to help people quit or reduce smoking
    northwesthealthline.ca
    , plus an online community and texting program for ongoing support
    northwesthealthline.ca
    . Phone: 1‑866‑366‑3667 (toll-free) connects to a quit coach in Ontario (generally available 7 days a week, with live phone coaching typically 10am–10pm)
    northwesthealthline.ca
    . Text: Send “iQuit” to 123456 for text-based support
    cancer.ca
    . Website: smokershelpline.ca – offers 24/7 online tools, forums, and live chat with quit coaches
    cancer.ca
    . Serves all Ontario (and Canadian) residents looking to quit tobacco; bilingual service (interpreters 100+ languages) and free of charge
    cancer.ca
    cancer.ca
    .

    Talk Tobacco – Specialized quit-smoking/vaping helpline for Indigenous peoples (First Nations, Inuit, Métis) in Ontario and select provinces. Provides culturally tailored, free and confidential support to quit commercial tobacco
    cancer.ca
    . Phone: 1‑833‑998‑8255 (toll-free, “TALK”) – available 7 days a week (hours vary by province, no service on statutory holidays)
    cancer.ca
    . Text: Send “CHANGE” to 123456 for text support
    cancer.ca
    . Offers services in English and French, with interpretation in 16 Indigenous languages
    cancer.ca
    . Website: talktobacco.ca.

Chronic Disease Information & Support Lines

    Diabetes Canada – Information & Referral Helpline – Toll-free diabetes information service. Provides resources, education, and navigation of diabetes programs/services for people living with diabetes or pre-diabetes (and their caregivers)
    211ontario.ca
    . Phone: 1‑800‑BANTING (1‑800‑226‑8464). Available Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm ET (business hours) for telephone or email inquiries (info@diabetes.ca)
    211ontario.ca
    . Serves all Ontario/Canada residents; bilingual support (English et français).

    Canadian Cancer Society – Cancer Information Service (Service d’information sur le cancer) – National cancer information helpline (toll-free). Trained information specialists provide answers on all types of cancer, prevention, treatment, and support resources
    cancer.ca
    . Phone: 1‑888‑939‑3333 (Mon–Fri, typically 9am–5pm)
    211ontario.ca
    . Also accessible via live chat and email on the CCS website
    cancer.ca
    cancer.ca
    . Serves anyone in Ontario/Canada – people with cancer, caregivers, or general public – with information and referrals to local services
    cancer.ca
    cancer.ca
    . Bilingual (English/French) with interpretation available
    cancer.ca
    .

    Lung Health Line (Lung Health Foundation) – Respiratory health information service. Staffed by Certified Respiratory Educators, it offers guidance on lung conditions like asthma, COPD, pulmonary fibrosis, smoking effects, etc.
    northeasthealthline.ca
    northeasthealthline.ca
    . Phone: 1‑888‑344‑LUNG (1‑888‑344‑5864) – Monday–Friday, 8:30am–4:30pm
    northeasthealthline.ca
    northeasthealthline.ca
    . Also responds to inquiries via email (info@lunghealth.ca). Serves people with lung health questions across Ontario; service in English and French
    northeasthealthline.ca
    .

    Arthritis Society – Arthritis Line – Telephone and email support service for people affected by arthritis. Trained volunteers provide information on arthritis types, treatment, pain management, and local resources
    arthritis.ca
    arthritis.ca
    . They can also refer callers to peer support if needed
    arthritis.ca
    . Phone: 1‑800‑321‑1433 (toll-free) – then press “2” to reach the Arthritis Line
    arthritis.ca
    . Email: info@arthritis.ca. Service is available Monday–Friday in both English and French (messages returned within 1 business day)
    arthritis.ca
    . Serves anyone in Ontario/Canada seeking arthritis info or support.

    Parkinson Canada – Information & Support Line – Helpline for people living with Parkinson’s disease, caregivers, and healthcare providers. Offers information about Parkinson’s symptoms, treatment options, community resources, and support programs. Phone: 1‑888‑664‑1974 (toll-free). Available Monday–Friday, 9am–4pm ET for live assistance
    parkinson.ca
    . Bilingual service (English/French); also offers email support and an online “CareFinder” tool. (Parkinson Canada’s helpline was formerly known as the Information & Referral Centre at 1-800-565-3000
    parkinson.ca
    , now consolidated under this support line.)

    Multiple Sclerosis (MS) Knowledge Network – National MS information and navigation service. Connects callers with MS Navigators who provide evidence-based information on multiple sclerosis, including disease management, treatment, research, and connections to local services
    mscanada.ca
    mscanada.ca
    . Phone: 1‑844‑859‑6789 (toll-free). Available Monday–Friday, 8am–8pm ET for phone consultations
    mscanada.ca
    . Also offers live web chat and email support (msnavigators@mscanada.ca)
    mscanada.ca
    . Bilingual (English/French) service covering all of Ontario/Canada. Note: MS Navigators are not crisis counsellors or medical providers (for urgent issues, individuals are directed to emergency services)
    mscanada.ca
    .

Health System & Public Health Information

    ServiceOntario INFOline (Ministry of Health) – General health system information line. A multilingual telephone service that provides information and referral regarding Ontario health programs and services
    southwesthealthline.ca
    . This includes inquiries about the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP), the Ontario Drug Benefit, assistive devices, long-term care, mental health services, etc.
    southwesthealthline.ca
    . Phone: 1‑866‑532‑3161 (toll-free) or 416‑314‑5518 (Toronto). Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30am–5:00pm ET
    southwesthealthline.ca
    . Service in English and French, with interpretation available in many other languages
    southwesthealthline.ca
    . (Note: This line is for non-clinical inquiries about health programs/policies; for medical advice use Telehealth/Health811.)

Caregiver Support

    Ontario Caregiver Helpline / Ligne téléphonique pour les soignants – 24/7 one-stop resource for caregivers across Ontario. Provides information, referrals and support to people caring for a family member, friend or loved one. Helpline staff (Community Resource Specialists) help connect caregivers to health services, home care, respite, support groups, and other programs in their local area
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    . Phone: 1‑833‑416‑2273 (1-833-416-CARE) – available 24 hours, 7 days a week
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    . Online Chat: available Monday–Friday, 7am–9pm ET via the caregiver website
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    . Services are free and confidential, in English or French (150+ other languages via interpretation)
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    . Note: This is an info/support line, not a crisis line (caregivers in crisis are directed to 911 or mental health crisis lines)
    ontariocaregiver.ca
    . Website: ontariocaregiver.ca (with chat and extensive resources).

Palliative Care Navigation

    Hospice Palliative Care Ontario (HPCO) Information Line – Provincial helpline for palliative care information and service navigation. Helps individuals, families, and caregivers find hospice palliative care services in their community
    hpco.ca
    . By calling, you can get assistance locating hospice programs, palliative care units, pain/symptom management services, bereavement support, and other end-of-life resources in Ontario. Phone: 1‑833‑621‑0728 (toll-free). Available Monday–Friday, 8:30am–4:30pm
    hpco.ca
    . Operated by HPCO; service is primarily in English (French resources available) – callers can also use thehealthline.ca directory for online self-search
    hpco.ca
    . (Not a clinical consult line, but for finding information and care options.)

Sexual Health Information

    Sexual Health Infoline Ontario (SHILO) – Free, anonymous sexual health information and referral line, province-wide. Provides confidential counselling and answers questions on topics such as sexually transmitted infections (STIs), HIV/AIDS (including PEP/PrEP), birth control, pregnancy options, safer sex, 2SLGBTQ+ sexual health, and clinic referrals
    toronto.ca
    toronto.ca
    . Phone: 1‑800‑668‑2437 (toll-free) or 416‑392‑2437 (Toronto) to speak with a live sexual health counsellor. Hours: Monday–Friday 10:00am–10:30pm; Saturday–Sunday 11:00am–3:00pm (closed statutory holidays)
    toronto.ca
    . Online eChat: available during the same hours (in English) via sexualhealthontario.ca
    toronto.ca
    toronto.ca
    . Services offered in English and French, with access to counsellors or interpretation in multiple other languages (Punjabi, Russian, Spanish, Swahili, Tagalog, Tamil, etc.)
    toronto.ca
    . This line is operated by Toronto Public Health but serves all of Ontario. (It is an info/support line and is not a primary crisis line or diagnostic service)
    toronto.ca
    .

Indigenous Health Access (Children & Families)

    Jordan’s Principle Call Centre – 24/7 federal helpline to ensure First Nations children get the health, social, and educational services they need without delays. Jordan’s Principle is a child-first initiative; families or service providers can call to request funding or access to needed health care, social supports, medical equipment, etc., for First Nations children in Ontario (and across Canada)
    fncaringsociety.com
    fncaringsociety.com
    . Phone: 1‑855‑JP CHILD (1‑855‑572‑4453) toll-free, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
    fncaringsociety.com
    . (TTY for hearing impaired: 1‑866‑553‑0554). Callers will be connected with an agent who can guide them through service requests under Jordan’s Principle and address urgent needs. Service is provided in English or French, with access to interpreters as needed. Website: canada.ca/jordans-principle – provides additional info and an online request form.

Each of the above services is free to use and has province-wide (or nationwide) coverage, ensuring that all Ontario residents – including specific populations like caregivers, Indigenous communities, or those with chronic illnesses – can get guidance and support by phone, and often by text or online chat. These resources are intended for non-emergency, non-crisis assistance with navigating health care, obtaining reliable health information, and connecting with appropriate services in Ontario.
