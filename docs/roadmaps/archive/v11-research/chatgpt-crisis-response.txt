Ontario-Wide Crisis Support Services (Phone, Text & Chat)

Below is a categorized list of free, province-wide crisis support services available to anyone in Ontario. Each service includes its English and French name (if applicable), contact information, a brief description, target audience, hours, and text/chat options. (Scope: “Ontario” means the service is provincial; “Canada” means it’s a national service accessible in Ontario.)
Suicide & Crisis Intervention

    988 Suicide Crisis Helpline / 9-8-8 : Ligne d’aide en cas de crise de suicide (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 988 (call or text). Website: [988.ca】
    canada.ca
    . A national 24/7 suicide prevention and crisis line offering confidential, bilingual (English/Français) support
    canada.ca
    . Serves anyone in emotional distress or thinking about suicide, of any age, anywhere in Canada
    canada.ca
    . Hours: 24/7 (365 days). Text: Text 988 (available 24/7)
    988.ca
    988.ca
    . (No sign-up needed; texting 988 connects you to the same helpline.)

    Distress and Crisis Ontario – ONTX (Online/Text Crisis Service) (Scope: Ontario) – Text: SUPPORT to 258258. Chat: [dcontario.org/chat][
    dcontario.org
    ]. A provincial online & SMS service offering emotional support and crisis de-escalation via trained responders
    dcontario.org
    . Serves any Ontario resident in distress or crisis (all ages). Hours: Daily from 2:00 PM to 2:00 AM EST
    dcontario.org
    . Languages: English (with French and other language capabilities via referral if needed). (ONTX partners with local distress centres to support users; standard texting rates may apply
    dcontario.org
    .)

Sexual Assault Support

    Male Survivors of Sexual Abuse – Provincial Support Line (Scope: Ontario) – Phone: 1-866-887-0015 (toll-free)
    cleoconnect.ca
    sadvtreatmentcentres.ca
    . A 24/7 multilingual crisis & referral line for male survivors of sexual abuse or assault in Ontario
    cleoconnect.ca
    sadvtreatmentcentres.ca
    . Provides immediate crisis counseling, emotional support and connection to specialized services (e.g. male survivor counseling programs) for men and boys who have experienced sexual violence (both recent or historical)
    cleoconnect.ca
    . Hours: 24/7, confidential. Serves: Any self-identifying male survivor, of any age, across Ontario (and can assist supporters of survivors). Languages: English and French, with access to interpreters as needed
    cleoconnect.ca
    . (Funded by Ontario Victim Services.)

    Victim Support Line (VSL) – (Scope: Ontario) – Phone: 1-888-579-2888 (toll-free)
    victimsupportdirectory.ca
    . Website: [victimsupportdirectory.ca】
    victimsupportdirectory.ca
    . A provincial 24/7 information and support line for victims of crime, including survivors of sexual assault and domestic violence
    ontario.ca
    . Trained staff provide emotional support, safety planning, and referrals to local services (e.g. sexual assault centres, victim services) anywhere in Ontario
    victimsupportdirectory.ca
    . Hours: 24/7 by phone. Chat: Online chat is available via 211 Ontario (Monday–Friday business hours) for those who prefer texting/chat
    victimsupportdirectory.ca
    . Languages: 200+ languages available through interpreters
    ontario.ca
    . (Operated by the Ontario Ministry of the Attorney General’s Victim Services. Not a counseling line but provides support and connections to help.)

Addiction & Substance Use Crisis

    ConnexOntario – Drug and Alcohol Helpline (Scope: Ontario) – Phone: 1-866-531-2600. Website: [connexontario.ca】
    connexontario.ca
    . (Same contact as ConnexOntario above.) This 24/7 helpline assists individuals facing substance use or addiction crises
    connexontario.ca
    . Trained specialists provide brief counseling, strategy suggestions, and referrals to addiction treatment or detox programs across Ontario
    connexontario.ca
    connexontario.ca
    . Hours: 24/7. Text: Text CONNEX to 247247 (24/7)
    connexontario.ca
    . Chat: Yes (24/7 via website). Who it serves: Anyone in Ontario dealing with alcohol or drug addiction concerns (self or others), including family seeking advice. (Service is free and confidential, in English or French, with interpretation available
    connexontario.ca
    .)

    National Overdose Response Service (NORS) (Scope: Canada) – Phone/Text: 1-888-688-6677 (call or text)
    nors.ca
    . Website: [nors.ca】
    nors.ca
    . A 24/7 virtual safe consumption hotline for people using drugs alone, to prevent fatal overdoses
    cowichan.pathwaysbc.ca
    . When you call or text NORS, a supportive peer stays on the line with you while you use, ready to call emergency services if you become unresponsive
    cowichan.pathwaysbc.ca
    . Hours: 24/7, confidential. Serves: Anyone in Canada who uses substances and wants someone to monitor their safety (and provide crisis support or overdose response if needed). Note: This service is free, anonymous, and non-judgmental, staffed by trained peers with lived experience
    cowichan.pathwaysbc.ca
    . (Not a treatment or counseling line, but a harm-reduction lifeline.)

    . Website: [kidshelpphone.ca】
    canada.ca
    . Canada’s 24/7 crisis line for youths offering free, confidential counseling and support on any issue

Indigenous-Specific Crisis Supports

    Hope for Wellness Help Line (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 1-855-242-3310 (toll-free)
    canada.ca
    . Website: [hopeforwellness.ca (for chat)】
    canada.ca
    . A 24/7 crisis counseling line for Indigenous peoples across Canada (First Nations, Inuit, and Métis) seeking emotional support, crisis intervention, or referrals
    canada.ca
    . Services are provided by Indigenous counselors with an understanding of cultural context. Hours: 24/7 phone support; Chat: 24/7 online chat counseling is available through the website
    canada.ca
    . Languages: English and French; on request, services are also available in Cree, Ojibway (Ojibwe) and Inuktitut
    canada.ca
    . Serves Indigenous individuals of all ages and their families.

    Talk4Healing – Indigenous Women’s Helpline (Scope: Ontario) – Phone/Text: 1-855-554-HEAL (1-855-554-4325)
    dcontario.org
    . Website: [talk4healing.com]. A 24/7 culturally grounded helpline for Indigenous women and 2-Spirit people in Ontario
    orcc.net
    dcontario.org
    . Offers counseling, crisis intervention, and support in 14 languages common to Indigenous communities (including Ojibway, Oji-Cree, Cree, etc.)
    dcontario.org
    . Hours: 24/7. Services: Phone or text the same number for support; online chat is also available via the website
    familieschange.ca
    . Who it serves: Indigenous women of all ages (and their families) dealing with violence, abuse, or any crisis. (“For Indigenous women, by Indigenous women” – provides a safe and healing space
    orcc.net
    .)

    National Indian Residential School Crisis Line (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 1-866-925-4419 (toll-free). A 24/7 crisis line for former residential school students and their families across Canada
    canada.ca
    . Provides emotional support and crisis counseling by counselors familiar with the trauma of residential schools. Hours: 24/7. Who it serves: Indigenous survivors of the Indian Residential School system and intergenerational family members. (Service available in English and French, with ability to connect to Cree, Ojibway, Inuktitut counselors as needed.)
    canada.ca

    Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women and Girls (MMIWG) Crisis Line (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 1-844-413-6649 (toll-free)
    canada.ca
    . A national 24/7 support line for those impacted by the issue of missing and murdered Indigenous women, girls, and 2SLGBTQQIA+ people
    canada.ca
    . Offers trauma-informed counseling, listening, and referrals. Hours: 24/7. Who it serves: Families, survivors, or anyone personally affected by MMIWG, including two-spirit and LGBTQ Indigenous folks
    canada.ca
    . (Bilingual service – English/Français – with additional cultural supports as needed.)

    sadvtreatmentcentres.ca
    . Website: [youthline.ca】. A peer-support helpline for 2SLGBTQ+ youth (age ~16–29) across Ontario
    211ontario.ca
    211ontario.ca
    . Trained LGBTQ2S+ volunteers offer confidential, non-judgmental listening, coming-out support, information on queer/trans resources, and just someone to talk to
    211ontario.ca
    211ontario.ca
    . Hours: Sunday to Friday, 4:00 pm – 9:30 pm EST (closed Saturdays)
    211ontario.ca
    211ontario.ca
    . Contact options: Youth can call toll-free during hours or text 647-694-4275 (SMS) for support
    sadvtreatmentcentres.ca
    . Chat: Yes – live web chat is available on the YouthLine site during helpline hours
    211ontario.ca
    . (Note: This is a peer support service for listening and referral; not a crisis intervention line

    . For 24/7 crisis help, youth can also contact Kids Help Phone or Trans Lifeline.)

    Trans Lifeline (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 1-877-330-6366
    translifeline.org
    sadvtreatmentcentres.ca
    . A hotline offering peer support by transgender people for transgender (and questioning) people in crisis or distress
    translifeline.org
    . The service is run by and for trans folks, providing understanding support for issues like gender dysphoria, isolation, self-harm, or suicidal feelings
    translifeline.org
    . Hours: Operators are on duty daily from 10 AM – 4 AM EST (approximately; this is 1 PM – 9 PM EST officially, with extended hours when possible)
    translifeline.org
    northwesthealthline.ca
    . Calls outside those hours may go to voicemail to request a callback. Who it serves: Transgender, non-binary, and questioning individuals across Canada. Languages: Service primarily in English (French support depends on volunteer availability). (Trans Lifeline does not trace or actively rescue callers without consent, aiming to offer a safe space to talk freely
    translifeline.org
    .)

    VAC Assistance Service / Service d’assistance d’ACC (Scope: Canada) – Phone: 1-800-268-7708 (toll-free). Website: [Veterans Affairs – mental health support】
    veterans.gc.ca
    . A 24/7 confidential counseling line funded by Veterans Affairs Canada
    veterans.gc.ca
    veterans.gc.ca
    . Provides immediate mental health support and crisis intervention for Veterans, Canadian Armed Forces members (current and former), RCMP members (former), as well as their families and caregivers
    veterans.gc.ca
    veterans.gc.ca
    . Hours: 24/7, 365 days a year
    veterans.gc.ca
    . Services: Phone counseling with a mental health professional, referral to local resources, and up to 20 sessions of free counseling if needed
    veterans.gc.ca
    veterans.gc.ca
    . Language: English or French (bilingual counselors available)
    veterans.gc.ca
    . Chat: An online chat appointment service is available (Mon–Fri) for non-urgent support scheduling
